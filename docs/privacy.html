Privacy Policy – Dial It Back

Last updated: May 2026

Dial It Back is a simplified Android launcher designed to make smartphones easier to use. This policy explains what information the app handles and how.

What the app stores

  • A contact list you create within the app (names and phone numbers)
  • A hashed admin PIN (if you choose to set one)
  • App preferences such as display settings, relaxed-mode app selections, and scheduling options

All of this data is stored on your device only. Nothing is uploaded to any server.

Contacts access

The app can optionally read your device's contacts so you can import names and numbers into the in-app contact list. This is a one-way import — data is copied onto the device's local storage and is not transmitted anywhere.

Phone and messaging

The app can initiate phone calls and open the messaging app on your behalf, using the system dialler and messaging apps already installed on the phone. The app itself does not record, log, or transmit call or message content.

What the app does not do

  • No data is sent to any remote server or third party
  • No analytics or tracking
  • No advertising
  • No account or sign-in required
  • No cloud sync or backup

Permissions

  • READ_CONTACTS – to import contacts from your device address book (optional feature)
  • CALL_PHONE – to make direct calls from the home screen without extra confirmation steps
  • POST_NOTIFICATIONS – to show scheduling reminders when the app switches modes
  • RECEIVE_BOOT_COMPLETED – to restore scheduled alarms after a phone restart
  • SCHEDULE_EXACT_ALARM – to trigger scheduled mode changes at a precise time
  • ACCESS_NETWORK_STATE – to check connectivity for diagnostics shown in the admin area

Children

This app is not directed at children and does not knowingly collect data from anyone under 13.

Changes to this policy

If the privacy practices of the app change materially, this page will be updated with a new date.
